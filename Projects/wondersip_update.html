Through the process of the brand revamp I noticed the product images of both our product listings and social media content needed reworking. A lot of the product images were lo-res photos of the straws and packaging.

For the product images I used 3D renders to have a realistic and higher quality image for customers you have a better impression of the product. I created a template of product images so that our product listings had consistent displays of the product.

I still included lifestyle photography so that the customer has an actual representation of the real life product. Due to time and budget constraints I composed the images the rely less on models and focused more on the product. Staging, props, and background were all thoroughly planned out.

UX/UI Design

I preformed an audit of the current Amazon Storefront and Company Website to see what can be improved for the user experience to hopefully drive sales. One thing the brand wanted to maintain was clear communication of how the product worked.

First I designed a sliding header. With the variety of sizes and colors in the product line, we thought it would be effective way to show off the products while including informational slides.

Much of our worry came across properly communicating how the product works. To solve that including transparent gifs of the product working.

Next, I analyzed the product page. With the upgrade to the newly rendered product images the page needed to be updated as well. Main objective was to make it easier for the user to navigate between all the variations of the product.

Finally, I updated the Amazon Storefront. This including updating imagery with custom updated brand assets such as infographics, headers, and product photography.
