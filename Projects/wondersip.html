WONDERSIP

OBJECTIVE

The objective was to build on the recent brand guidelines update by making sure all brand assets were aligned with the new direction. This included updating the website, Amazon storefront, and all photo and video content to reflect the refreshed look and messaging. The process involved reviewing previously existing assets and making design or content changes where necessary to stay consistent with the updated guidelines. It was all about creating a more unified and professional brand experience across every customer touchpoint.

PRODUCT IMAGES

Through the process of the brand revamp I noticed the product images of both our product listings and social media content needed reworking. A lot of the product images were lo-res photos of the straws and packaging.

For the product images I used 3D renders to have a realistic and higher quality image so customers can have a better impression of the product. I created a template of product images so that our product listings had consistent displays of the product.

I still included lifestyle photography so that the customer has an actual representation of the real life product. Due to time and budget constraints I composed the images to rely less on models and focused more on the product. Staging, props, and background were all thoroughly planned out.

SOCIAL MEDIA CONTENT

In tandem with the brand revamp the new marketing and social media content had to use the same visual identity. This involved working with our marketing team and planning all new content.

A way to allow the marketing team some autonnomy and allwo them to plan some content without me, I created an all new library of decorative graphics, 3D renders, and downloadable typeface.

INSTAGRAM

UX/UI DESIGN

I performed an audit of the current Amazon Storefront and Company Website to see what can be improved for the user experience to increase traffic and drive sales. One thing the brand wanted to maintain was clear communication of how the product worked.

WEBSITE

AMAZON

First I designed a sliding header. With the variety of sizes and colors in the product line, we thought it would be effective way to show off the products while including informational slides.

Much of our worry came across properly communicating how the product works. To solve that I included transparent gifs of the product working also effectively played into the tag line often used in marketing "Snap, Clean, and Click".


Next, I analyzed the product page. With the upgrade to the newly rendered product images the page needed to be updated as well. The main objective was to make it easier for the user to navigate between all the variations of the product.

Finally, I updated the Amazon Storefront. This including updating imagery with custom updated brand assets such as infographics, headers, and product photography.
